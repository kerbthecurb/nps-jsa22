Destinations to visit during the

Mid Autumn Festival!



Mid Autumn Festival is all about the reunion of families and friends. It is celebrated all around Vietnam, from the most rural areas such as upper-Nothern Vietnam, to the urban areas of Southern Vietnam; each place with various destinations to visit along the way. So, what are you waiting for? Here are my top 10 picks for this Mid Autumn Festival!


________________________


1. Hang Ma Street, Ha Noi



From a long time back, Hanoi Old Quarter became super energized and colorful whenever it came to the 8th month of the lunar calendar. People from all over the nation assemble to celebrate Mid the autumn festival which makes the streets much busier than on a  typical day. As the night falls, Hang Ma Street becomes exceptionally beautiful with thousands of shimmering lights of all shapes and colors. During the Mid-Autumn Festival, Hang Ma Street is the place where you can discover all sorts of toys, from old school to present-day stuff, this will make your children excited and they will enjoy one of the biggest celebrations in Vietnam.  




Hang Ma Street filled with toys and decorations.

________________________



2. Imperial Citadel of Hanoi ( Hoàng Thành Thăng Long )



The Majestic Citadel of Thang Long (or Hanoi Citadel) is a complex of notable royal buildings found within the center of Hanoi, Vietnam. It was first built amid the Ly dynasty when they moved to Hanoi in 1010. Having existed for over a thousand years, numerous structures inside were torn down and vanished, but within the early 2000s, the Hanoi Citadel was efficiently unearthed. Now it has become a favorite destination for both local people and guests, and many important ceremonies and celebrations were held here, including the Mid – Autumn festival. 




The Citadel under the light of day.

________________________



3. Hoi An, Quang Nam



What do we have in Hoi An during Mid – Autumn Festival? Hoi An is astonishing on each and every full moon and it is indeed more fabulous in Mid – Autumn festival, typically also a highlight when you travel to Hoi An. You may feel the true feeling of an antiquated festival here. Each road, each corner is colorful and engaging with lights, blessings, and decorations. Unlike everywhere else, Mid-Autumn in Hoi An is exceptionally serene. Thousands of people line up across the river to observe the drifting candles, take a few photographs and remember the great old days. 




Hoi An the night of the festival.

________________________



4. Tuyen Quang City, Tuyen Quang



Last but not least, is Tuyen Quang City. In case you have never heard about Tuyen Quang City, do not stress since it isn't a popular destination for foreign visitors. Interestingly, Tuyen Quang holds the biggest Mid-Autumn Festival celebration in Vietnam and attracts thousands of Vietnamese visitors all over the nation. Tuyen Quang City is popular since people there celebrate the Mid-Autumn Festival pretty seriously with numerous intriguing activities. Thousands of individuals of all ages and genders will walk on the road alongside tremendous lights of one-of-a-kind shapes and colors just like the parade. There are several gigantic lanterns. They are made with steel outlines that weigh up to 100 kilograms, greater than a whole bus. There are a few exotic shapes of the lights too, for example, animals, national heroes, etc. Individuals celebrate the Mid-Autumn Festival in little groups everywhere else, but not here, in Tuyen Quang, the entire city will celebrate this together.
